Quiche Lorraine

Quiche is a French tart consisting of pastry crust filled with savoury custard and pieces of cheese, meat, seafood or vegetables.The best-known variant is quiche Lorraine, which includes lardons or bacon. Quiche can be served hot or cold. It is popular worldwide.

Bacon Quiche Lorraine

6 servings
    1 9-inch store-bought pastry shell
    6 slices bacon - cooked and crumbled
    1/4 cup onion - chopped
    1 and 1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
    4 large eggs - lightly beaten
    1 and 1/3 cup milk
    3/4 teaspoon salt
    1/2 teaspoon dry mustard
    1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    1/8 teaspoon pepper

Procedure :

  • Preheat oven to 230 degrees Celcius.
  • Spread some parchment paper / oil & dust the baking tray with flour
  • Bake pastry shell for five minutes then remove from oven and set aside.
  • Reduce the oven temperature to 190 degrees Celcius
  • In a frying pan over medium heat, saute the onions until soft and translucent.
  • In medium bowl, mix the eggs with milk and all seasonings.
  • Sprinkle the onion and crumbled bacon into the pastry shell.
  • Cover with the cheese and pour egg mixture over the cheese.
  • Bake for 40 minutes until egg is completely set.
  • Drive a toothpick into the center, if comes out clean, the quiche is done.

Quick Tip :

To prevent the edges of the crust from becoming too well-done, tear off thin strips of aluminum foil and place them over the edges of the pastry shell before placing the constructed quiche in the oven.
